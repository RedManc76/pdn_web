Bring us into greater intimacy with the Spirit; break our hearts for what breaks yours.

Help us to appreciate the preciousness of your intimate presence and jealously protect it with every last drop of strength.

Share in the intercession of Christ by his Spirit for what he wants to do through us.

Grant us the faith to know that we have received your commission correctly and that you have heard our prayer, and we will see the answer.

May our faith not falter and cause us to cease before we have what we are asking for.

That you will cause our faith to grow as we experience increasingly powerful answers.

Reveal to us what is the biggest hindrance in our lives to true prayer.

Give us a hatred of those things in our lives that hinder prayer and cause us to fall up in your mercy until they are eradicated.

Give us a greater zeal to see our brothers powerfully used.

See the cross in all its glory.

Give us a heart that longs for God's glory, and everything we do is done for that chief end.

Conform our hearts, minds, and wills to your word and grant us a greater zeal to know it and a greater love to apply it.

Increase our love for one another, prefer one another, and desire them to be used more than ourselves.

Protect our unity in prayer from the schemes of the evil one.

Grant us a greater love for prayer and communion with the living God than anything else in our lives.

Each new member is prepared, joins the right group, and grows in their understanding of the seriousness of this work and their usefulness to it.

Prepare leaders to lead in accordance with our values, in great solemnity, and to facilitate the work of the Spirit.

New gatherings will be planted and used mightily for the Lord's glory.

The gospel goes out not only in word but in power, transforming lives, pulling down strongholds, and glorifying Christ.

The local church grows in love, holiness, and power. Sinners are to be added to the church, discipled, and sent out.

Revival in our nation: the kingdom of Christ advanced, and the kingdom of darkness crushed under the foot of the church with Christ as its head.
